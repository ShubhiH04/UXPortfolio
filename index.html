InterpretAid

The Problem

Most of the time, patients misunderstand medical terminology during healthcare visits, leading to medication errors and poor health outcomes. ESL speakers, elderly patients with cognitive challenges, and those with hearing impairments face even greater communication barriers.

My Role & Process

As one of our team's UX designers, I researched 3 existing health/translation related tools, mapped out ethical considerations, and designed multiple iterations with a Value-Sensitive Design-Based Approach through wireframing and high-fidelity prototyping in Figma.

Solution & Key Features

An AI-enhanced mobile communication tool that provides real-time medical term simplification, visual aids, and multi-modal support including voice-to-text transcription, symptom logging, and personalized health literacy resources.

Key Learnings & Design Validation

Design decisions were grounded in accessibility best practices, addressing tech literacy levels, privacy and hospital policies, and most importantly, patient growth.The multi-modal approach (visual, audio, text) addresses diverse learning styles identified in research on health literacy. This project strengthened my skills in designing for vulnerable populations and balancing AI capabilities with user trust. Next steps would include usability testing with 10-15 patients across different demographics to validate the term simplification accuracy, adjust the interface based on cognitive load feedback, and even start ideating about what integrating AI would actually look like!

MarketMate

The Problem

Health-conscious consumers struggle to make informed grocery decisions due to complex nutrition labels, lack of dietary filtering options, and disorganized grocery lists (which are usually mental notes!!). Users needed a streamlined way to search products, understand nutritional content, and manage shopping lists efficiently.

My Role & Process

As a UX designer and developer of our team, I conducted competitive analysis of 3 existing grocery shopping apps, created user flows and wireframes, and helped develop the full application using React and Firebase. I integrated Open Food Facts API to provide comprehensive dietary information, and worked on aligning the API's input with our front-end visuals, specifically edge-cases where images or dietary data were not in the API's database.

Solution & Key Features

A responsive web application featuring advanced product search with dietary filters, detailed nutrition breakdowns, favorites management, and collaborative shopping list creation. Users can compare products side-by-side and save personalized preferences.

Technical Achievement & Potential Impact

Successfully developed and deployed a fully functional web application integrating real-time API data from Open Food Facts' database of over 1 million products. The app demonstrates practical application of React state management, Firebase authentication, and responsive design principles. Given the rising interest in health-conscious eating (60% of consumers report checking nutrition labels), this tool addresses a clear market need and could serve health-conscious shoppers seeking efficient grocery decisions.

Revivo

The Problem

Humanity produces 62 million tonnes of electronic waste every year (UNEP). UW students with damaged devices lack guidance on whether to repair, replace with used devices, or upgrade, leading to premature disposal and environmental impact. Washington's Right to Repair Bill created an opportunity for better decision-making tools.

My Role & Process

As a UX designer and Researcher of the team, I conducted 3 student interviews to understand device decision-making patterns, performed competitive analysis of repair services, and created 3 user personas representing different budget and sustainability priorities. Developed information architecture and prototyped multiple user flows in Figma.

Solution & Key Features

A mobile app that guides students through repair vs. replacement decisions based on device condition, budget constraints, and environmental impact. Features include damage assessment wizard, cost comparison calculator, local repair shop integration, appointment scheduling, and device lifecycle tracking.

Impact & Future Potential

UW users appreciated the multiple decision pathways and the overall tackling of the decision rationale issue. My next steps for this project would be partnering with UW Facilities to pilot the app and measure actual repair rates.

When AI Lies With Confidence

The Problem

Large Language Models (LLMs) can generate convincing but inaccurate health information, particularly dangerous in women's reproductive health where misinformation leads to real-world harm. Users lack tools to assess AI response credibility, and confident-sounding answers appear more trustworthy regardless of accuracy.

My Role & Process

As the sole developer on a 4-person research team, I designed and implemented the technical infrastructure using Python, LangChain, and machine learning models. Analyzed over 4,000 LLM responses, web-scraped NIH databases for accuracy verification, and developed a RandomForestClassifier to predict confidence vs. accuracy patterns. Created Chrome extension for real-time LLM scanning.

Solution & Technical Implementation

Built a machine learning pipeline that scores LLM responses for both confidence and accuracy, creating a visual "trust matrix" for users. Developed Chrome extension that alerts users when AI responses show high confidence but low accuracy. Used Flask API, HTML/JavaScript for the interface, and integrated NIH fact-checking.

Impact & Findings

Conducting a Chi-Squared Statistic Test revealed a P-Value 0.05, meaning that there is a relationship between accuracy and confidence level. Model achieved 90% accuracy in predicting misinformation. Findings presented at the iSchool Research Showcase, earning a Visionary Lens Award of Insightfulness. Tool empowers users to critically evaluate AI health information, potentially preventing medical misinformation spread.
